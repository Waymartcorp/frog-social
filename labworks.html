LabWorks is a bench-side appendix to Frog Social: focused on in vitro fertilization, frozen sperm, oocytes and ion channels, extract preparation, early embryology, and advanced work like nuclear transfer. This page is a scaffold for shared, evolving best practices — not a formal protocol book, though labs are invited to have their protocols hosted here.

Always cross-check with your institutional protocols and published methods. Values below are typical starting points, not a substitute for local optimization.

Solution Recipes (working starting points)

Exact formulations vary across labs. These are “typical” versions — document your local variants and measured pH/osmolarity.

Barth’s / Modified Barth’s (example 10× stock)

10× Barth’s stock (dissolved in distilled water, pH adjusted to ~7.4–7.8):

ComponentApprox. concentration (10×)
NaCl880 mM
KCl10 mM
MgSO₄·7H₂O25 mM
Ca(NO₃)₂·4H₂O8 mM
CaCl₂·6H₂O10 mM
NaHCO₃250 mM
HEPES100 mM

Working Barth’s / MBS: dilute 1:10 with distilled water for 1×. Re-check pH after dilution. Many labs add antibiotics or tweak osmolarity — if you do, write it down.

0.1× MMR (common embryo medium)

ComponentApprox. concentration (0.1×)
NaCl10 mM
KCl0.2 mM
MgSO₄0.1 mM
CaCl₂0.1 mM
HEPES5 mM (pH ~7.8–7.9)

Often prepared as a 10× stock and diluted 1:10. Record which recipe you used, the pH, and any additives.

ND96 (typical oocyte recording buffer)

ComponentApprox. concentration
NaCl96 mM
KCl2 mM
CaCl₂1.8 mM
MgCl₂1 mM
HEPES5 mM (pH ~7.5)

Variants include antibiotics or modified divalents. For troubleshooting, log which variant you used, pH, and change frequency.

Oocyte Storage & Care

“Bad oocytes” often come from subtle storage issues: solution pH drift, temperature instability, infrequent changes, or poor hygiene.

Checklist for storage conditions

  • Solution: ND96 variant, additives (antibiotics? serum?), target pH.
  • Temperature: actual °C (measured), not just “room temp”.
  • Change frequency: e.g. full change daily vs every 2 days.
  • Dish density: number of oocytes per dish and volume of medium.
  • Hygiene: how often dead/degenerating oocytes are removed.

Visual checks

  • Spherical, not collapsed or irregular.
  • Even animal/vegetal polarity; no darkened patches.
  • No fungal fuzz, debris, or surface film.

Cell-Cycle Extract Preparation (troubleshooting view)

Extract quality is extremely sensitive to timing, temperature, pH, and shear. This section emphasizes variables to record whenever performance changes.

Key timing points

  • Hormone injection → egg laying (hours, temperature).
  • Egg collection → dejellying (minutes).
  • Dejellying → first spin / extract processing (minutes).

Cysteine & handling

  • Cysteine concentration and exact pH (e.g. 2% w/v, pH 7.8–8.0).
  • Duration of exposure and mixing intensity.
  • Buffer formulation and temperature during egg handling.

Mechanics & centrifugation

  • Egg pack conditions (g-force, time, rotor type).
  • How aggressively extracts are triturated or mixed.
  • Which steps are cold vs room temp, and how that is enforced.

In Vitro Fertilization

IVF outcomes depend on egg quality, sperm handling, medium, and temperature. LabWorks is a place to capture how your “good days” differ from “bad days.”

Domains to log

  • Time from stripping eggs to sperm exposure.
  • Fertilization medium (Barth’s / MMR variant) and measured pH.
  • Temperature at fertilization and early cleavages.
  • Method: “mash cloud” vs prepared sperm suspension.
  • Age/condition of males and females.

Frozen Sperm Protocols

Frozen sperm is unforgiving of temperature and timing mistakes. This section is about making thaw and use conditions explicit.

Checklist ideas

  • Cryoprotectant and freezing schedule (high level).
  • Thaw temperature and duration (actual numbers, not “quickly”).
  • Dilution buffer composition and time before contacting eggs.
  • Expected fertilization rate window when things are working.

Priming & Hormone Use

Priming schedules differ substantially between labs. Here the goal is to record what you actually do and how animals look/perform, not to impose one recipe.

  • Hormone type, dose, route (e.g. hCG, GnRH analogs).
  • Interval from priming → booster → collection.
  • Differences between under- and over-primed animals.
  • Patterns in long-term fertility after repeated priming.

Early Embryology & Tadpole Development

Connect bench decisions (media, pH, temperature, handling) to developmental windows where embryos most often get into trouble.

Common failure windows

  • 0–12 hours: fertilization failure, polyspermy, extreme pH/osmolarity.
  • Gastrulation / neurulation: pH drift, temperature shifts, disturbance.
  • Later larvae: food type/size, underfeeding, poor oxygenation, waste buildup.

Nuclear Transfer & Advanced Manipulations

Nuclear transfer and related micromanipulations are high-skill domains. Rather than a full protocol, this section highlights process checkpoints worth logging.

  • Enucleation method and how success is verified.
  • Donor nucleus handling: medium, temperature, and time.
  • Micromanipulator calibration and ergonomics (drift, backlash).
  • Culture conditions post-transfer and survival benchmarks by stage.

LabWorks is intentionally a “fun added bones” layer: a place to grow collective protocol intelligence without confusing it with welfare endpoints or formal veterinary guidance.
